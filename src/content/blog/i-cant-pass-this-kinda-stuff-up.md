Monday morning. Tennessee Picker Kevin sent us three Enamans — those custom Funko-style figurines whose capes claim *we get things moving* — and I had a goofy idea before pulling the first order: pick a stuck item, put an Enaman on it, see if it sells.

## The experiment

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9lflGphm4xg&t=120s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[2:00]</a></strong> I put one on the Diaz Home mugs (already pretty safe, honestly), Candice put one on the lobster utensil holder, and we put a third on the Pee-Wee Herman puzzle. *"All right. You get to work, sir. Get things moving."* If they all clear by the next video, Kevin's about to get a bulk order — *"if it works, I'm going to buy 100 more."*

## Order pulling — eBay, Mercari, Poshmark

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9lflGphm4xg&t=240s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[4:00]</a> Steve Madden women's sneakers — $34.99.</strong> *Bought by Nikki T from Rev Resale.* Lock me in. Five years ago, Rev bought a pair of Italian driving shoes from us based on me hyping them up on a video. He kept the bad-shoe grudge until the podcast and finally let it out. So Nikki T buying these shoes is her way of throwing us an olive branch. Rev: *don't try them on. Don't even hold them. And Nikki T — even if they hurt, don't tell him.*

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9lflGphm4xg&t=625s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[10:25]</a> Bram Stoker "Jewel of the Seven Stars" 1904 first American edition — $119.99.</strong> Same sale where we picked up the John Dixon Carr first editions. Black linen-covered hardback — sat on Six Bravo, not Three Bravo where I tried to pull it. Big-money books *do* tend to migrate to Six Bravo somehow.

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9lflGphm4xg&t=720s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[12:00]</a> Four Stanley nesting cookware sets + a magnet — about $84.</strong> One viewer order. *"Clearly that's a viewer sale."* Thank you. We started with around 72 of these, we're down to about 60.

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9lflGphm4xg&t=1056s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[17:36]</a> Unicron cold-cast Botcon statue — over $200.</strong> The kicker: I forgot to put the word *Transformers* in the title. *"Wait, Lonnie. Where do you…"* Probably why it took so long to sell. We need to audit the whole store for that.

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9lflGphm4xg&t=1568s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[26:08]</a> Three poster lots, all to one buyer — Atari Force ($29.36), Sundevils ($14.88), Banners Galaxy ($11.88).</strong> 50 days ago we bundled a bunch of unsellable single posters into lots. This buyer made offers on three at once. Two of those lots were so obscure I half-suspect we threw them in for free. Cash in hand, shelves clear. *"We left a little money on the table, but we do that often."*

## The garage sale at the end

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9lflGphm4xg&t=2155s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[35:55]</a> Halo 3 SPNKR "Spanker box" — $1.</strong> Sunday morning hit on the way home from another sale. The lid lifts open like a little case — disc went in one slot, books went in the other. Last three sold comps: $55, $65, $75. We almost walked away because I thought it was missing the locks — and the lady at the sale, somehow, *heard* me say that from across the yard, dug into another box, and pulled out the locks. Showed me how to clip them on. For a one-dollar item.

That's a $1 → $50+ flip because somebody at a garage sale was kind enough to fish around in another box for a part she had no obligation to find.

Other picks from that sale:
- **Penny Dreadful Clue** — keeping for myself, $15.
- **Royal Doulton Hampshire dinnerware** — $20 for a partial set (5 dinner plates, 7 salad, 12 saucers, 2 bowls, 10 cups). Probably $150 listed in parts.
- **Bookshelf for Boys & Girls** — 10-volume hardcover set, $10.
- **Programmed Classics** — 14 hardcover novels, $8. Saw a 16-set sell for $90.

## Wrap

The viewer-bought Stanley sets, the Nikki T olive branch, and a stranger digging through a box for the locks on a $1 game case — that's the day. Pippen's nowhere in this video. The Enaman experiment is in progress. Bram Stoker is on a truck somewhere.
