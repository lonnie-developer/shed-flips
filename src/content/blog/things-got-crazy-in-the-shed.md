Welcome back to the shed. This post is a walk-through of one of our shipping days—the kind where the thing that feels chaotic on camera is actually the routine we've built around listing and fulfillment.

## What we cover here

We pull orders from eBay and Mercari, show you what sold, talk through our listing pace, and share some lessons we learned (or re-learned) about shipping logistics and platform quirks while moving inventory.

## How much we're listing

Before we jump into individual orders, Lonnie had been keeping a tally of our daily listing totals. The numbers matter because they show the rhythm we're hitting: we've been listing somewhere between $500-$700 most days, with the occasional sourcing day that pulls us away. We've got enough inventory on hand that we *could* list that much every single day indefinitely — which is the goal.

The feeling when you hit that groove is different from scrambling. We're not racing to fill a gap; we're moving through what we've got at a pace that feels sustainable.

## The shipping day logistics

Today was designated a shipping day, which means pulling orders that sold on eBay and Mercari, packing them, and getting them out. It's the platform-agnostic part of the job — whether an item sold for $15 or $150, it needs to get boxed and dropped.

One thing that got brought up during the pull was our plan to try ListEase (a third-party eBay tool). We've been threatening to switch for a while, and the main roadblock was that ListEase requires you to use eBay's business policies for shipping. We made the switch last week, and immediately found out why we'd resisted before.

**The gotcha:** When you set up a shipping method in business policies, it doesn't show you the estimated total shipping cost in real-time. So Lonnie accidentally entered a 15-pound weight on one listing when the actual package was 2 pounds. He didn't catch it until he went to list the same item on another platform and the shipping estimate looked wrong.

It's the kind of thing that catches you once and you never forget — you just get more careful. The range eBay shows you is wide (it has to account for worst-case scenarios like express shipping to Guam), so you can't always eyeball it. Check your weights.

## The orders

The orders that came through on this day included:

- A belt buckle on eBay (multi-item order, first item)
- Multiple small finds that got lot together
- A couple of what Lonnie called "fella backs" — items that were solid purchases at the right price point with room to profit

One of the highlights was a pair of cowboy boots that we'd scored for a solid price at a garage sale. The play on those: buy at the right price, list in the fastest way possible (not perfect condition notes, not a gallery of 12 photos), move on. If it sells for what you listed it at or better, you've won.

Another was a lot-style listing — grouping items together when individually they wouldn't be worth the listing effort, but as a bundle they make sense. Lonnie had picked up some gear, comped a few comparable listings, and decided bundling would move it faster than parceling everything out.

## The lesson

The title of this video — "Things got kinda crazy in the shed today" — isn't about chaos. It's about velocity. You get a shipment day where you're pulling orders, handling multiple platforms, keeping track of weights and dimensions, catching your own mistakes before they ship, and moving good inventory out the door. To someone outside the shed, that looks chaotic. To us, it's the system working.

The rhythm we've built lets us source without sacrificing fulfillment, list without scrambling, and move inventory without overthinking it. You buy at the price that makes sense, list in the way that moves it fastest, and ship it out. Some days that's exciting finds; some days it's quiet hustle. Both matter.

- ListEase and similar tools exist to speed up the listing process, but they're only useful if you understand the platform underneath them.
- Bundling slow-moving inventory into lots is an old strategy that still works — especially on eBay and Mercari.
