This is the first chunk of our Aida Springs Citywide footage. The whole sale is too big to cover in one video — we'll be threading it through a couple. The two things worth pulling out from this round: the picks, and the moment we got recognized at a sale for the first time ever.

## A few highlights from the haul

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nqLO0QhaUes&t=63s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[1:03]</a> Tommy Hilfiger spell-out jacket — $5.</strong> Lady selling: *"I just want it out of here. I'm not taking nothing back."* That's our favorite kind of seller. Liner is still zip-tied — may not have even been worn. We're guessing $75 list.

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nqLO0QhaUes&t=125s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[2:05]</a> OEM floor mats, two sets — $15 total.</strong> 2019 Ford Explorer set (new in tag, the seller's wife had WeatherTechs over them) and a used set for a 2019 F-150. We've sold a dozen sets of mats over the years and they always sell — average $40-50, more for fancy trim packages. Easy money.

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nqLO0QhaUes&t=797s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[13:17]</a> Five National Shrimp Festival pottery mugs — $1 each.</strong> Numbered out of 150, by an artist named Burrow, dated 1991 through 1995 — *consecutive years*. We could only find one comp on eBay; Candice may try them at $25 a whack just to see what the market is.

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nqLO0QhaUes&t=917s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[15:17]</a> Hot Kiss jeans with lips on the back pockets — $2.</strong> Candice spotted those. *"Kiss My Grits."* Mel's Diner. We'll see.

## Meeting Emily

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nqLO0QhaUes&t=470s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[7:50]</a></strong> Emily walked up at one of the sales and recognized us. *"Y'all are my number one."* I was so amped I asked her if my hair looked crazy — which is a strange thing to say to a person you're meeting for the first time, but I had no warning and we don't usually get recognized. So Emily, if you're watching: I'm sorry I was weird. That's just what you get with me.

She did the selfie thing with us and posted it in the Reseller Water Cooler Facebook group later. That was a first for us. It made our day.

## Order pulling — eBay and Mercari

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nqLO0QhaUes&t=1140s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[19:00]</a> Set of 4 Spode Buttercup mugs — $100.</strong> Estate-sale buy at $10. One of them has a misprint flaw — the stamp didn't hit clean. Candice spotted it during photos and disclosed it in the title. I'd have shipped it without ever noticing.

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nqLO0QhaUes&t=1414s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[23:34]</a> Pee-Wee Herman 1000-piece puzzle — $19.99.</strong> Sale to Sarah, a long-time viewer who asked us to sign the box. We'll do that during the pack video. Thanks Sarah.

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nqLO0QhaUes&t=1685s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[28:05]</a> Whistle Stop Cafe Mysteries 13-book lot — $54.99.</strong> Garage-sale buy at $8 a few days back. Book lots are heavy and bulky, but when they're priced right at sourcing, they pay for the floor space.

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nqLO0QhaUes&t=1731s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[28:51]</a> Stephen King "Cycle of the Werewolf" Signet first edition — $19.99.</strong> Same buyer also grabbed a magnet.

<strong><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nqLO0QhaUes&t=1746s" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[29:06]</a> Dr Seuss 23-book hardcover lot — $39.99.</strong> Listed for $40, paid $30 for this and another lot we listed at $20 — so $30 in for $60 in potential listings. Honestly not the smartest buy. Probably $5–$20 profit after fees. Candice called it as we listed: *"that's not really the best business."* Live and learn.

Plus a Mercari sale — Holly Holiday Fiesta candle holders for $59.99. Best pieces in that set we picked up. People ask whether you should bother listing Christmas stuff in the spring; the answer keeps being yes.

## Wrap

That's the first slice of Aida Springs. Meeting Emily was the moment, the Spode catch was the reminder that Candice sees stuff I don't, and the Dr Seuss buy was the reminder that being excited about a book lot doesn't always mean it's a smart book lot. More from this sale to come.
